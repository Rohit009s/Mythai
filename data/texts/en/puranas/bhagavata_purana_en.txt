The Bhagavata Purana, also known as Srimad Bhagavatam, is one of Hinduism's eighteen great Puranas. It promotes bhakti (devotion) to Krishna as the path to moksha.

The text consists of twelve books (skandhas) totaling 18,000 verses. The tenth book, which describes Krishna's childhood and youth, is the most popular and widely studied.

Structure:
- Books 1-2: Cosmology and creation
- Books 3-4: Avatars and devotees
- Books 5-9: Various avatars of Vishnu
- Book 10: Life of Krishna (most extensive)
- Books 11-12: Philosophy and prophecies

Key Teachings:
- Supreme devotion (Bhakti) to Krishna
- The nine forms of bhakti
- Krishna as the Supreme Personality of Godhead
- Stories of devotees like Prahlada, Dhruva
- The Rasa Lila and Krishna's divine play

The Bhagavata Purana is considered the most popular and influential of all the Puranas, and has inspired countless commentaries, translations, and devotional movements.
