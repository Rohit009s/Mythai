The Shiva Purana is one of eighteen Purana genre of Sanskrit texts in Hinduism, and part of the Shaivism literature corpus. It primarily revolves around the Hindu god Shiva and goddess Parvati, but references and reveres all gods.

The Shiva Purana contains chapters with Shiva-centered cosmology, mythology, relationship between gods, ethics, Yoga, Tirtha (pilgrimage) sites, bhakti, rivers and geography, and other topics.

Key Sections:
- Vidyeshvara Samhita: Creation and cosmic principles
- Rudra Samhita: Birth and life of Shiva
- Satarudra Samhita: Manifestations of Shiva
- Kotirudra Samhita: Four disciples of Shiva
- Uma Samhita: Glory of goddess Parvati
- Kailasa Samhita: Shiva's abode and worship

The text emphasizes that Shiva is the supreme being, the destroyer and transformer within the Trimurti. It teaches that through devotion to Shiva, one can attain moksha (liberation).

Important Stories:
- Marriage of Shiva and Parvati
- Birth of Ganesha and Kartikeya
- Destruction of Daksha's sacrifice
- The churning of the ocean
- Shiva's cosmic dance (Tandava)
