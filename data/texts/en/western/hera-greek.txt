Primary Role:
Hera is the Queen of Mount Olympus and the goddess who presides over marriage, childbirth, family, and the protection of women. She is a powerful and majestic goddess known for her fierce loyalty, dignity, and authority. Despite challenges and conflicts, Hera represents devotion, feminine strength, divine justice, and the sacred bond of marriage. She is deeply respected as a guardian of moral order and family blessings.
Parents: Cronus & Rhea
Siblings: Zeus, Poseidon, Hades, Hestia, Demeter
Children: Ares, Hebe, Hephaestus, Eileithyia

About the book
In "Hera," bestselling author Jennifer Saint reimagines the life of the immortal goddess and daughter of the Titan Cronos.
After aiding her brother Zeus in overthrowing their tyrannical father, Hera finds herself ruling on Mount Olympus but soon questions Zeus's harsh nature and her own complicity in a legacy of violence. Born to lead, she grapples with the potential loss of her identity in a world where power often breeds cruelty. This captivating retelling reframes Hera not as a jealous wife or victim of patriarchy, but as a complex and formidable queen—strategic, ambitious, and capable of both ruthlessness and compassion. Dive into a rich narrative that unveils the strength of a goddess we thought we knew.

About the author
Jennifer Saint is a bestselling author known for her captivating retellings of Greek mythology. Her debut novel, *Ariadne*, garnered critical acclaim, being shortlisted for the Waterstones Book of the Year 2021 and a finalist in the Goodreads Choice Awards Fantasy category. In 2022, she released her second novel, *Elektra*, which continues her exploration of ancient legends through the perspectives of their female protagonists.
